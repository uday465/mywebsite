UAE Visa Types We Support

Tourist Visa (30/60/90 days)

For leisure, sightseeing, and short visits to Dubai and the UAE. Duration and entry rules depend on the package you choose and sponsor type.

Transit Visa

For travelers passing through the UAE with a short stop. Ideal when you need to leave the airport or stay briefly between flights.

Employment Visa

Sponsored by a UAE employer. Includes work permit and residence procedures handled by your company or PRO.

Mission Visa (Business)

For short business assignments, meetings, or project work sponsored by a UAE entity. Not a long-term work residence by itself.

UAE e-Visa System & How to Apply

The UAE issues most visit visas as e-visas linked to your passport. You typically apply before travel; processing is often 3-5 business days (varies by channel and nationality).

Common application channels:

  • Airlines — Many carriers (e.g. Emirates, Etihad, flydubai) offer visa services when you book eligible tickets.
  • Authorized travel agents — Licensed agents submit applications on your behalf with the required documents.
  • GDRFA — General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (e.g. Dubai GDRFA) portals are used for certain visa types and sponsor workflows.
Visa on arrival: Some nationalities may obtain a visa on arrival or use simplified entry rules. Requirements change — always confirm with your airline, the UAE ICA/GDRFA, or an authorized agent before you travel.

UAE Travel & Visa — Dos & Don'ts

Do's

Respect local customs and the dress code

Dress modestly in public areas, especially malls, government buildings, and religious sites. When in doubt, err on the conservative side.

Carry a printed copy of your e-visa

Keep a clear printout or PDF of your approval with your passport. Airlines and immigration may ask to see it at check-in or arrival.

Register on GDRFA when required

If your sponsor or visa type asks for GDRFA registration or updates, complete them promptly to avoid entry or stay issues.

Have confirmed return or onward tickets

Immigration expects evidence you will leave before your visa expires. Keep bookings that match your stated itinerary.

Follow Ramadan etiquette in public

During Ramadan, avoid eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours out of respect for those fasting.

Use only authorized channels to apply

Book through recognized airlines, licensed agents, or official portals to reduce fraud and rejection risk.

Don'ts

Don't carry prohibited items

UAE laws are strict on drugs, certain medications without documentation, and banned goods. Check customs rules before you pack.

Don't overstay your visa

Leaving after your permitted stay triggers fines and can affect future entry. Plan extensions only through legal channels before expiry.

Don't use a VPN for illegal activities

VPN misuse to access illegal content or commit offenses is risky. Use the internet responsibly and in line with UAE regulations.

Don't ignore public behavior rules during Ramadan

Loud music or inappropriate behavior in public during Ramadan can cause offense. Be discreet and follow local guidance.

Don't work on a tourist or visit visa

Paid employment requires the correct work permit and visa. Mission and employment categories exist for legitimate business and jobs.

Don't rely on unofficial visa “guarantees”

No agent can guarantee approval. Avoid fake documents or misstated travel reasons — penalties are severe.

Step-by-Step UAE Visa Application Process

Step 1

Choose Your Visa Type and Duration

Decide whether you need a tourist (30, 60, or 90 days), transit, employment, or mission (business) visa. Duration and fees (often AED 350-1000 depending on type and stay length) vary — confirm with your sponsor or channel.

Validity: 30/60/90 days depending on type
Step 2

Apply Online via Authorized Agent, Airline, or GDRFA

Submit your application through an authorized travel agent, participating airline (with eligible booking), or relevant GDRFA / official portal workflow. No embassy interview is required for standard e-visas — processing is document-based.

Step 3

Upload Your Documents

Provide passport scans, photos, flight and hotel details, and any supporting documents (bank statements, NOC, invitation) as requested. Ensure names and dates match your passport exactly.

Step 4

Pay the Visa Fee

Pay the applicable fee (typically in the AED 350-1000 range based on duration and channel) via the agent’s, airline’s, or portal’s payment method. Keep the receipt or transaction reference.

Fee varies by duration and visa type
Step 5

Receive Your e-Visa by Email

After review, your e-visa is usually emailed within 3-5 business days (can vary). Check spam folders and verify all details match your passport before travel.

Typical processing: 3-5 business days
Step 6

Print and Carry Your e-Visa

Print a clear copy of your e-visa and carry it with your passport. Present it at airline check-in and immigration if asked. Plan to complete any GDRFA or arrival steps your sponsor instructed.

Frequently Asked Questions — UAE Visa

An e-visa is approved before you fly and sent by email, usually applied through an airline, authorized agent, or sponsor-linked process. Visa on arrival applies only to certain nationalities and situations at the border — rules change, so always confirm with your airline and official UAE sources before traveling.
Extensions depend on your visa type and sponsor. Some visit visas can be extended through authorized channels before expiry; others cannot. Never overstay — apply or exit before your permitted date.
The UAE charges daily overstay fines once your visa or grace period ends. Amounts and grace rules are set by authorities and can change. Pay fines through official channels when exiting or regularizing status — check current rates with GDRFA/ICA or your sponsor.
If you stay airside within the airport and your nationality allows, you may not need a transit visa for a short connection. If you plan to leave the airport or your itinerary requires it, apply for a transit visa through your airline or agent in advance.
Dress modestly in public areas: cover shoulders and knees where practical, especially in malls, metro, and government buildings. Beachwear belongs at the beach or pool. During Ramadan, extra care applies in public behavior and clothing — when unsure, choose conservative options.
